THE HANNIBAL

DEFENSIVE PLAYBOOK

Inspired by the Battle of Cannae. We do not honey-pot around the ruck. We set up wide, defend from the outside-in, and force the attack back into the heavy traffic in the middle of the pitch.

RULE 1

Defend the OUTSIDE SHOULDER.

RULE 2

Line speed is dictated entirely by the SCOUT.

RULE 3

Stop the wrap-around, force them back inside.

Role Clarity & Responsibilities

GUARDS 1 & 2

Pillar & Post

  • • Guard the immediate ruck fringes and call the "Lift".
  • • Seek double tackles to choke tackle, rip the ball, or drive them back 5 metres.

THE NAUSE

Ruck Disruptor

  • • Entire job is to fiercely contest the ruck.
  • • Slow down their 9 or turnover weak sealers.

THE WOLF

4th Defender (Inside Center)

  • • Aggression. Do not wait.
  • • Ignore dummy runners and HUNT the "Ace" (opposition playmaker).
  • • Completely shut down their ability to move the ball wide.

THE SCOUT

Last Defender / Edge

  • • Stand WIDE. Dictate the line speed for the whole team.
  • • Show the sideline to deny the step back inside.
  • • Force them to pass under pressure or cut back into the Wolf.

PENDULUM

9, 10, 15

  • • 9: Sweeps shallow to clean up chip kicks or line breaks.
  • • 10 & 15: Operate a deep pendulum to cover long kicks.
  • • Do not shoot up early—prevents exposing the Kill Zone behind the line.

THE FLAG

Secret Role

  • • A covert assignment given before each match to a chosen few.
  • • Specific match-day duties determined by the coaching staff.

System Vulnerabilities (Avoid)

Honey-Potting: Getting narrow around the ruck concedes massive edge overlaps. Offside Starts: Start legally behind the hindmost foot to read the attack. Disconnected Wolf: If the Wolf shoots and the Scout stays deep, it creates a fatal dog-leg. Move as a connected wall.
